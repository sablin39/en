What is URA?

To encourage our undergraduate students to be engaged in research work under the supervision of faculty advisors, CUHK(sz) has introduced the Undergraduate Research Awards (URA) Programme since 2016. This is the 20th round.

Abstract

Inspired by the wide usage of DQN and game theory in multi-agent decision process, I hereby propose a motion planning strategy that can help a team of agents survive while protect themselves in a dynamic environment with another team of threatening intelligent agents. The agents will be limited by a set of rules, which are similar to competitive video games. In the research, we gradually develop a strategy based on deep neural network. We will first develop a simple agent strategy to find an appropriate way to construct a cost map based on the threat of hostile agents; then we will add multi-agent methodology in order to make cooperation possible and improve the overall performance of each agent team. Finally physical rules will be added to make the environment more realistic. As an outcome, we can develop a motion planning strategy based on DQN which can guide agents to cooperate and win human players. The strategy should also work in physical environments after modifying the trajectory of bullets.

Introduction

The motion planning process of agents has been a heated topic in robotic fields. Many have put up algorithms which aimed at making decisions to automatically explore dynamic environments[^1] or achieve specific goals in a competitive game (Mnih et al., 2015). Many of them adopt deep neural networks to observe high-level environment and act as action-value function.
Inspired by the wide usage of DQN and game theory in multi-agent decision process, I hereby propose a motion planning strategy that can help agents survive while protect themselves in a dynamic environment with threatening intelligent agents, which is different from the Atari 2600 games since the “AI” in these games will not evolve by interacting with players.
